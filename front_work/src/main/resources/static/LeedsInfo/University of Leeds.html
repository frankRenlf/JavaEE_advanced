University & LEEDS

The University of Leeds, is one of the first-class, top-notch and largest universities in the UK

History

The predecessor of Leeds University is Leeds Medical College, which was founded in 1831, and Yorkshire College of science, which was founded in 1874. In 1887, the above two schools merged with Manchester Erwin college and University of Liverpool college into Vitoria United University. In 1904, Leeds University became an independent university with royal permission. After more than a century of development, Leeds University has become one of the most famous universities in the UK.

Achievement

Leeds University is one of the top 100 universities in the world and one of the top ten research universities in the UK; among the universities in England, Leeds University is one of the universities that provide the most courses; the library has more than 2.5 million books; the school has more than 200 student associations; and the university has more than 200 student associations;

Honor

A total of six scientists have won the Nobel Prize.
