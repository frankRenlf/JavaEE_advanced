Economy of Leeds





Leeds is the second largest financial center in the UK. The Bank of England (the Central Bank of England) has offices in Leeds. There are more than 30 headquarters or branches of financial institutions such as banks and building societies in the urban area (three of the five largest building societies in the UK are located in Leeds, managing a total of 60 billion pounds of assets). Leeds's financial institutions provide 128000 jobs, contributing more than 10 billion pounds a year to the UK economy (2015)

Leeds is the UK’s fastest growing city and is the main driver of a city region with a £64.6 billion economy, a combined population of 3 million and a workforce of 1.37 million.

Over the next ten years, the economy is forecast to grow by 21% with financial and business services set to generate over half of GVA growth over that period.

- Household income in Leeds, is 93% more than it is in London and is 8% above the National Average.
- London unemployment rate is 5.80%.
- Leeds unemployment rate is 2.90%.

Bank of England

The Bank of England is the central bank of the United Kingdom. Sometimes known as the 'Old Lady' of Threadneedle Street, the Bank was founded in 1694, nationalised on 1 March 1946, and gained independence in 1997. Standing at the centre of the UK's financial system, the Bank is committed to promoting and maintaining monetary and financial stability as its contribution to a healthy economy.
